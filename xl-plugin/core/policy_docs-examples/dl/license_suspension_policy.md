# License Suspension Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Issuing Authority:** Department of Motor Vehicles

## Section 100 — Purpose and Scope

This Policy establishes the conditions under which a driver's license shall be suspended or revoked based on the accumulation of demerit points and the severity of recent violations. It applies to all holders of a Class C or Class M driver's license.

This Policy operates in conjunction with the Traffic Violation Code and incorporates by reference the Severity Class definitions and demerit point values established in that Code (see Traffic Violation Code, Sections 300 and 400).

## Section 200 — Definitions

**Recent Violation.** Any violation for which demerit points were recorded against the driver's license within the preceding twenty-four (24) months from the date of assessment.

**Weighted Point Total.** The sum of demerit points from all Recent Violations, adjusted by the recency weight defined in Section 300.

**Severity Class.** As defined in the Traffic Violation Code, Section 300. This Policy treats Severity Class as authoritative and does not redefine it.

**Action Tier.** The administrative consequence triggered by a driver's Weighted Point Total and Severity Class history, as set forth in Section 400.

## Section 300 — Weighted Point Accumulation

The Weighted Point Total is computed by applying a recency weight to the demerit points of each Recent Violation, based on the number of months elapsed since the violation was adjudicated:

| Months Since Adjudication | Recency Weight |
|---------------------------|----------------|
| 0–6                       | 1.0            |
| 7–12                      | 0.75           |
| 13–18                     | 0.5            |
| 19–24                     | 0.25           |
| 25+                       | 0 (excluded)   |

The weighted contribution of a violation is its demerit points (as determined by the Traffic Violation Code) multiplied by its recency weight. The Weighted Point Total is the sum of these weighted contributions across all Recent Violations.

### Section 310 — Severity Class Escalation

In addition to the Weighted Point Total, the presence of Recent Violations of Severity Class C or D escalates the assessment as follows:

- A single Recent Violation of Severity Class D triggers immediate review under Section 400, regardless of the Weighted Point Total.
- Two or more Recent Violations of Severity Class C within a 12-month window trigger immediate review under Section 400, regardless of the Weighted Point Total.

## Section 400 — Action Tiers

Based on the Weighted Point Total and any Section 310 escalations, the Department shall assign one of the following Action Tiers:

| Action Tier   | Trigger                                                                 | Consequence                                  |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| **None**      | Weighted Point Total below 6.0 and no Section 310 escalation             | No administrative action                     |
| **Warning**   | Weighted Point Total at or above 6.0 but below 10.0                      | Written warning; mandatory defensive driving course within 90 days |
| **Suspension**| Weighted Point Total at or above 10.0, OR Section 310 escalation present | License suspended for 90 days                |
| **Revocation**| Weighted Point Total at or above 16.0, OR two or more Severity Class D Recent Violations | License revoked; minimum 1-year ineligibility for reinstatement |

A higher tier supersedes a lower tier when multiple triggers apply.

## Section 500 — Reinstatement

A suspended license is automatically reinstated at the conclusion of the suspension period, provided the driver has completed any required defensive driving course and paid all outstanding fines under the Traffic Violation Code, Section 500.

A revoked license may be reinstated only after:

1. The minimum ineligibility period has elapsed,
2. The driver has completed a Department-approved driver re-education program, and
3. The driver has passed both the written and road examinations.

## Section 600 — Notice and Appeal

The Department shall notify the driver of any Action Tier assignment in writing within ten (10) business days of the triggering assessment. The driver may appeal the assignment within thirty (30) days of notice. During the pendency of an appeal, a Suspension or Revocation tier shall be stayed unless the assignment was triggered solely by Section 310 (Severity Class D escalation), in which case the action takes effect immediately.
