# MS 523. INCOME ELIGIBILITY

## A. INCOME ELIGIBILITY

A case is income-eligible for Medicaid when the applicant's countable monthly income is less than the applicable income standard for the household size and eligibility category.

### A.1. Gross income

Gross monthly income is the sum of the applicant's gross monthly earned income (wages, salaries, self-employment net) and gross monthly unearned income (Social Security, pensions, child support).

### A.2. General income disregard

A standard $20 general income disregard is subtracted once from gross monthly income (not separately from each income source) before comparing to the income standard. The disregard never reduces countable income below zero.

### A.3. Income standard

Select the applicable monthly income standard from the program standards table (Addendum 1) using the eligibility category and household size as keys. The table supports household sizes 1 through 8.

### A.4. Decision

When countable monthly income is strictly less than the applicable income standard AND the applicant is a U.S. citizen (or qualified non-citizen) AND a current Alaska resident, the case is authorized. Otherwise the case is denied, with the denial reason set per MS 521 (citizenship), MS 522 (residency), or A.4 (income over standard).
